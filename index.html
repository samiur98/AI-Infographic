Crypto Currency

Intro

Crypto Currencies are a collection of digitilized monetary entities that have generated a great deal of excitement in the tech community. It is a revolutionary technology that is both frightening and exciting at the same time. It is also a technology that threatens to change our economic infrastructure. The question now being whether this change is a positive or a negative. This infographic is not designed to answer this question but rather provide some general information to help readers make an informed decision.

History

Before this infographic dives into the technical mechanics of cryptocurrencies, it is important that readers have some knowledge of the history of cryptocurrencies. Although cryptocurrencies have gained prominence fairly recently through the success of bitcoin, the invention of cryptocurrencies date back as early as 1983. The invention can be credited to American cryptographer David Chaum. However, he technically did not invent a cryptocurrency but the first form of digitilized money. He called it ecash. The key breakthrough of Chaum's invention was the fact that it enabled untracebale payments. This is also a property that cryptocurrencies have inherited. The first decentralized cryptocurrency was invented in 2009, by a programmer/s by the pseudoname Satoshi Nakamoto. This cryptocurrency was named BitCoin and the identity of its creator/s remains a mystery to this day. Soon after other cryptocurrencies such as LiteCoin followed. Despite functionaly being the same technology, there are certain differences in the implementation of different cryptocurrencies. For example BitCoin uses the SHA-256 hashing algorithm while LiteCoin uses the scrypt hashing algorithm. Despite many competitors BitCoin remains the most popular and valuable cryptocurrency.

Mechanics

In this section the infographic will try to explain the underlying mechanics of how cryptocurrencies work in a simple and non-jargon fashion. The key question I believe that most readers are interested in is how cryptocurrencies generate value. There are many complex topics that work under the hood such as peer to peer networks, public ledgers/blockchains, and hashing algorithms. The key thing to understand is that cryptocurrencies generate value by rewarding computers/machines for solving computational problems. Unlike traditional fiat money, the creation and distribution is not controlled by a centralized authority. Whether this is a positive or a negative is up to the reader to determine. As far as usage is concern, cryptocurrencies are reknown for their security but they require the use of computer technology. This may not seem like an issue in first world countries like America and Canada but for now it is something that may not be feasible for the global community. This is particularly true for countries and communities that are lacking in terms of finance and technological education.

Mining

Mining is the process by which users may recieve cryptocurrencies. During the mining processs, a machine/computer attempts to solve a computational/hashing problem. Cryptocurrencies are then rewarded based on the success of their machine/computer. Although the premise of mining is simple, the process and strtegies involved are quite complex. There are many factors to consider such as the machine being used, the electrical consumption and cost of the electrical consumption, and the market value of the cryptocurrency being mined. Readers are advised to do research and/or consult experts before diving into mining cryptocurrencies. This article is a good starting point for any readers who may be interested.

Key Positives of Cryptocurrencies:

  • Decentralization prevents monopoly/abuse of power
  • Secure transactions

Key Negatives of Cryptocurrencies:

  • Decentralization may cause chaos/economic instability
  • Cryptocurrency mining is a complicated process
  • Requires the use and understaing of basic technology
